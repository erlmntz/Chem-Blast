🧪 How to Play

          

            
Goal: Balance the chemical equation by placing the correct coefficients (numbers) into the empty boxes.

            

            
Step-by-step example:

            

              _ H2 + _ O2 → _ H2O
            

            
    
              
  1. Count atoms on each side:
     Left: H=2, O=2  |  Right: H=2, O=1
    2. 
              
  2. To balance oxygen, put 2 in front of H2O:
    
                _ H2 + _ O2 → 2 H2O
    3. 
              
  3. Now right side has H=4, O=2. Balance hydrogen by putting 2 in front of H2:
    
                2 H2 + _ O2 → 2 H2O
    4. 
              
  4. Now left H=4, O=2; right H=4, O=2 → balanced!
    
                Final: 2 H2 + 1 O2 → 2 H2O
    5. 
            

            
Drag the numbers from the tray at the bottom into the empty slots. If all slots are filled correctly, you score!
